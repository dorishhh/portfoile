Temperature Analysis

Background Information:

Wireless temperature and indoor air quality sensors were installed in spaces confirmed in-use during Summer 2018 to help evaluate each buildings’ system performance and to diagnose potential issues and health hazards. The data was compared against the established building standards of WELL and ASHRAE for the best design and performance-based, data-driven measures and metrics.


Key Observations:

  • Most spaces are not within desired temperature and humidity comfort range;
  • Engineers on-site only care about maintaining 60-80°F temp. levels as opposed to 68-72°F comfort levels;
  • High particulate matter, CO2, dust, VOCs were observed in both Dickey Hill & Cecil Elementary.
Wireless temperature and humidity sensors were installed in several rooms
Wireless indoor air quality (IAQ) AWAIR sensors were also installed in a few select rooms to measure volatile organic compounds (VOCs), CO2, particulate matter, and dust levels (in addition to temperature and humidity).

Summary of Min/Max Temperatures Observed

Some spaces, such as large auditorium, were cooled to maintain temperatures in the 60s°F – overworking the chiller leading to shorter equipment life. Classrooms had average temperatures of ~80°F making concentration difficult for students & teachers.

Undercooling in Spaces

Dickey Hill did not have proper cooling with the window A/Cs to maintain temperatures in the desired comfort range.

Undercooling and Overcooling in Spaces

Cecil Elementary was observed to have spaces both too hot and too cold for determined comfort levels of 68 – 72 F.

High humidity level in Dickey Hill

High humidity leads to mold and respiratory health issues.

High humidity level in Cecil

High Volatiel Organic Compounds(VOCs) Levels in Cecil Elementary

Spikes in VOCs may be due to cleaning supplies, paint, or construction.

High Dust Level in Dickey Hill-Classroom 115

High dust levels could be from drywall, cleaning, or moving furniture.

High CO2 Level in Dickey Hill-Classroom 114

This is a result of inadequate ventilation for when the classrooms are occupied.

Overcooling inside rooms of Cecil Elementary

Overcooling results in wasted energy and is uncomfortable for students and teachers. It was observed on site that even unused spaces are kept with A/C on constantly since anything below 60 F is “not critical".

Heat Pump + Solar

Air-source heat pumps (ASHPs) provide space heating and cooling. They make use of a vapor compression cycle to move heat from indoors to outdoors (during summer) and from outdoors to indoors (during winter). In effect, it is an air conditioner that can run in reverse during cold weather.

Heat Pump Operations:

The advantages of heat pumps: use electricity to move heat versus converting electricity directly to heat, thus, can be up to four+ times more efficient than electric heat do not utilize combustion, so no chimney or venting is required newer cold-climate air sourced heat pumps (ccASHPs) can match and deliver heating loads at 5 F and sometimes at -13 F



Comparative analysis of economic potential from three diverse types of fuel-fired heating systems


A baseline is assumed to be the existing equipment midway through its useful life. Fuel switching opportunities from oil fired boiler to ccASHPs are most favorable, and ccASHPs should be recommended when replacing an older natural gas fired boiler equipment reaching the end of its useful life.

Project Financing

OBR

  • Loans from investors are repaid through the energy bills
  • debt service from energy finance loan is added to monthly utility costs
  • loans are used to cover energy project costs, utility bills significantly decrease (15-30%)
  • Decrease in Utility Bills + Debt Service = Positive Net Savings
  • Places the utility bills and debt service on the same note which significantly decreases the risk that debt service will not be paid

Equipment Leasing

  • the overall risk of construction cost and loan defaulting decreases
  • the construction risk is mitigated by contractor bidding package of projects
  • lowers the risk of unexpected cost increasing during construction
  • investors also have access to diverse energy projects to invest according to their preferences
  • building owners is motivated with more flexible financing options and lower interest rate to install energy efficiency projects
